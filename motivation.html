Chhichhore (2019)

143 minutes 8.3/10
Comedy Drama College Life

School and college life are times we never forget—moments of joy, mischief, and friendship that bring smiles and sometimes tears when recalled. Campus life in hostels is particularly special, with experiences you can't get anywhere else.

Much like the beloved film "3 Idiots," "Chhichhore" brings a fresh perspective on friendship and life's challenges. The story revolves around six misfits and one girl who create an unforgettable bond during their college years.

Each character has their unique quirks: Sexa obsessed with magazines, Acid failing in class but expert in cursing, Mummy the coward, Derek the unofficial father figure always with a cigarette, the alcoholic whose room has more bottles than books, and Anni, our serious yet adventurous protagonist. And there's Maya, the campus crush everyone turns to look at twice.

12th Fail (2023)

125 minutes 8.9/10
Biographical Drama Perseverance

The emotional impact of this film is beyond words. It effortlessly delivers a powerful message: education is the most valuable asset one can possess.

Some may say, "What's the point of studying? What good is a degree? Look at me—I'm doing better than those with degrees without having studied." Don't listen. Never stop your education—its importance will become clear sooner or later.

Let's continue working toward what we haven't yet achieved. Don't give up—keep holding on. If your intentions are good and you're not causing harm to anyone, you will definitely succeed one day.

3 Idiots (2009)

170 minutes 8.4/10
Comedy Drama Education

The film that revolutionized how we think about education and success. "3 Idiots" follows three engineering students as they navigate through a rigid educational system that values grades over understanding.

Rancho's philosophy of "pursue excellence, and success will follow" challenges conventional thinking about achievement and purpose. Through laughter and tears, this film delivers profound wisdom about following your passion and questioning established systems.

More than a decade after its release, the film's message remains relevant as we continue to question what true education means and how success should be measured in life.

Taare Zameen Par (2007)

165 minutes 8.3/10
Drama Education Special Needs

This heartwarming film tells the story of Ishaan, a young boy with dyslexia who is misunderstood by everyone until he meets an art teacher who recognizes his unique abilities and challenges.

"Taare Zameen Par" powerfully demonstrates how each child learns differently and has their own special talents. The film criticizes the one-size-fits-all education system that fails to nurture individual capabilities and interests.

Beyond being just an emotional journey, this film serves as an important reminder for parents and educators to look beyond academic performance and recognize the diverse ways children express their intelligence and creativity.
